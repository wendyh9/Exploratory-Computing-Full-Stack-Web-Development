Beef Rendang

Beef Rendang is a spicy, rich Malaysian/Indonesian beef stew made with beef, coconut milk, and an assortment of spices. It's arguably the most famous beef dish in Malaysia, Indonesia, and Singapore. There are variations of this dish, such as chicken rendang and lamb rendang. This dish is best eaten with rice, especially nasi lemak (a fragrant Malaysian coconut milk rice).

Ingredient List

Ingredients Measurements
stewing beef 1kg
chopped red chiles 6 pieces
desiccated coconut 1 cup
chopped onions 2
chopped garlic 6 cloves
sliced galangal 1/2 thumb size
sliced ginger 1/2 thumb size
finely chopped lemongrass 1 stalk
finely chopped tumeric leaf 1
sliced lime leaves 6 pieces
curry powder 4 tbsp
beef stock 1 cup
coconut milk 1 cup
dark soy sauce 1 tbsp
oil 3 tbsp
salt 1 tbsp

Steps

  1. In a blender pour the oil then add the chillies, onion, garlic, galangal, ginger and lemongrass, blend to a paste. Set aside.
  2. In a pan add desiccated coconut and lightly dry toast over medium heat. Once toasted remove fom pan, let it cool then place in a food processor to grind. Set it aside.
  3. In a heavy pot fry the spice paste, lime leaves and turmeric leaves over medium high heat. Stir continuously for 3 minutes or until very fragrant.
  4. Add the beef then continue to stir fry until beef is covered with the spices evenly for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the ground desiccated coconut, curry powder, coconut milk, soy sauce and beef stock. Bring to a boil and simmer in very low heat for 2 hrs or until sauce dries out and beef is tender.
